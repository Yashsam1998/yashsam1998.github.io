About.

I am "Batch of 2020" student who has majored in Chemical engineering with a minor in Electrical engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Mumbai. My upbringing in culturally diverse metros has shaped me as a person with many unique perspectives and ideas. The inflow of ideas has kept me curious to learn more, which is satiated not only by reading but also by experimenting. I am a Tinkerer and strive to learn by building. My name is Yash Samriya.

I find Chemical Engineering a fascinating field to be working in due to its wide variety of applications in day to day lives. I have got opportunities to improve process efficiencies by applying chemical engineering, working in FMCG, auto manufacturing, and oil refinery. I have explored the medical-devices field. I engineered a portable, low-cost, point-of-care blood-electrolyte measurement device to cater to remote Indian villages where an affordable medical diagnosis is not available.

During my involvement with the Aeromodelling club in my undergraduate studies, I have experimented with RC Planes, multi-rotors, ornithopters, kites, and the electronics involved in the field. People refer to me as an "Organization Freak" due to my obsession with cleanliness, lists, and time-tables.
